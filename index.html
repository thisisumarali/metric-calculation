Long Ton: - A long ton, also known as the imperial ton or UK ton, is a unit of mass commonly used in the United Kingdom and some other British Commonwealth countries. - 1 long ton is equal to 2,240 pounds (approximately 1,016.05 kilograms). 2. Short Ton: - A short ton, also known as the US ton or customary ton, is the standard unit of mass in the United States. - 1 short ton is equal to 2,000 pounds (approximately 907.18 kilograms). 3. Metric Ton: - A metric ton, also known as a tonne, is the standard unit of mass in the metric system and is widely used internationally. - 1 metric ton is equal to 1,000 kilograms (approximately 2,204.62 pounds). In summary, the main difference between these ton units is their mass equivalent. The long ton is larger than the short ton and the metric ton. The short ton is used in the United States, while the metric ton is used in most other parts of the world and is the standard ton unit in the International System of Units (SI).
